[§124B-73] Contempt; authority to punish; punishment. (a) With respect to any proceeding under this chapter: (1) A military judge detailed to a court-martial or any other proceeding under this chapter; (2) Any military magistrate designated to preside under section 124B-46; or (3) The president of a court of inquiry, may punish for contempt any person who conducts themselves in violation of section 710-1077. (b) The punishment for contempt under subsection (a) shall be the same as the punishments permitted under civilian criminal contempt of court laws and rules. (c) A punishment imposed under this section: (1) If imposed by a military judge or military magistrate, may be reviewed in the same manner as review from the circuit courts of the State; and (2) If imposed by a court of inquiry, shall be subject to review by the convening authority in accordance with rules adopted by the governor or adjutant general, or as provided by law.
