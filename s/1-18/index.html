§1-18 "Or", "and". Each of the terms "or" and "and", has the meaning of the other or of both. [PC 1869, c 1, §4; RL 1925, §15; RL 1935, §16; RL 1945, §15; am L 1945, c 233, §2; RL 1955, §1-23; HRS §1-18] Case Notes Applied: 230 F. Supp. 455; 17 H. 135, 141; 34 H. 935, 941. "And" deemed conjunctive, not alternative. 33 H. 98, 101. "Or" deemed alternative, not conjunctive, penal statute. 44 H. 601, 604, 359 P.2d 289. Applied in substituting "and" for "or" in public accountancy statute. 51 H. 80, 452 P.2d 94. "Or" deemed "and". 54 H. 356, 507 P.2d 169. Use of word "and" is not necessarily decisive. 56 H. 675, 548 P.2d 268. Cited: 52 F.2d 411, 414; 5 H. 52, 53; 19 H. 393, 397. Previous Vol01_Ch0001-0042F Next
