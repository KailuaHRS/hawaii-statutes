Establishment of parent-child relationship.

⚖ Official version ↗
PART III. PARENT-CHILD RELATIONSHIP [§584A-301] Establishment of parent-child relationship. A parent-child relationship is established between an individual and a child if: (1) The individual gives birth to the child, except as otherwise provided in part IX; (2) There is a presumption under section 584A-303 of the individual's parentage of the child, unless the presumption is overcome in a judicial proceeding or a valid denial of parentage is made under part VI; (3) The individual is adjudicated as a parent of the child under part V; (4) The individual adopts the child; (5) The individual acknowledges parentage of the child under part IV, unless the acknowledgment is rescinded under section 584A-403(f) or successfully challenged under part IV or V; (6) The individual's parentage of the child is established under part VIII; or (7) The individual's parentage of the child is established under part IX.
