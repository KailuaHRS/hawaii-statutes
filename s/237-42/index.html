APPEALS §237-42 Appeals. Any person aggrieved by any assessment of the tax for any month or any year may appeal from the assessment in the manner and within the time and in all other respects as provided in the case of income tax appeals by section 235-114. [L 1935, c 141, §1; am L 1937, c 202, §1; RL 1945, §5473; am L 1953, c 125, pt of §10; RL 1955, §117-37; am L Sp 1957, c 1, §29; HRS §237-42; am L 2000, c 199, §3; am L 2004, c 123, §3] Case Notes Taxpayer's 42 U.S.C. §1983 claim against validity of Hawaii's general excise tax barred, where state remedies available to taxpayer were "plain, adequate, and complete". 940 F. Supp. 260 (1995). Prepayment requirement is constitutional. 57 H. 1, 548 P.2d 246 (1976). Previous Vol04_Ch0201-0257 Next
