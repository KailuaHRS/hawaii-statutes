§502-53 No certificate of acknowledgment contrary hereto valid in court or entitled to be recorded; exception. No certificate of acknowledgment contrary to this chapter is valid in any court of the State, nor is it entitled to be recorded in the bureau of conveyances, but no certificate of acknowledgment executed before July 29, 1872, shall in consequence of anything in this chapter contained be deemed invalid. [L 1909, c 69, §9; RL 1925, §3158; RL 1935, §5144; RL 1945, §12744; RL 1955, §343-37; HRS §502-53] Case Notes Cited: 26 H. 121, 123 (1921). Previous Vol12_Ch0501-0588 Next
