[§708-812.5] Burglary offenses; intent to commit therein a crime against a person or against property rights. A person engages in conduct "with intent to commit therein a crime against a person or against property rights" if the person formed the intent to commit within the building a crime against a person or property rights before, during, or after unlawful entry into the building. [L 2006, c 230, pt of §2] COMMENTARY ON §708-812.5 Act 230, Session Laws 2006, added this section, defining the phrase "with intent to commit therein a crime against a person or against property rights."
