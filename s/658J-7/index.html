[§658J-7] Motion for judicial relief. (a) A motion for judicial relief under this chapter shall be made to the court in which a proceeding is pending involving a family law dispute subject to arbitration or, if no proceeding is pending, a court with jurisdiction over the parties and the subject matter. (b) Upon motion of a party, the court may compel arbitration if the parties have entered into an arbitration agreement that complies with section 658J-5 unless the court determines under section 658J-12 that the arbitration should not proceed. (c) Upon motion of a party, the court shall terminate arbitration if it determines that: (1) The arbitration agreement is unenforceable; (2) The family law dispute is not subject to arbitration; or (3) Under section 658J-12, the arbitration should not proceed. (d) Unless prohibited by an arbitration agreement, upon motion of a party, the court may order consolidation of separate arbitrations involving the same parties and a common issue of law or fact if necessary for the fair and expeditious resolution of the family law dispute.
